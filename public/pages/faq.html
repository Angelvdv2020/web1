How does ZoogVPN work?

ZoogVPN ensures a secure and private online experience by creating a protected connection between your device and the internet. Let’s see how it works step by step:

  • Device initialization. The user installs and activates a VPN client on their device, which establishes a connection to the VPN server.
  • Encryption. ZoogVPN client encrypts the user’s internet traffic using protocols like OpenVPN, IKEv2/IPsec, or WireGuard. This ensures that the data is unreadable to third parties.
  • Tunneling. Encrypted data is encapsulated in a secure “tunnel” that connects the user’s device to the VPN server.
  • IP masking. The VPN server assigns the user a new IP address, replacing the original one to anonymize the user’s location.
  • Data transmission. The VPN server decrypts the incoming traffic, forwards it to the target destination (e.g., a website), receives the response, encrypts it, and sends it back to the user’s device.
  • Decryption on device. The VPN client decrypts the incoming data so the user can interact with it as usual.

With ZoogVPN, you can browse, stream, and communicate with complete privacy and peace of mind.

How can I switch country on Apple Store?

Here’s a concise guide to switching your country or region on the Apple Store:

  1. Cancel all your current subscriptions before proceeding to change the region; make sure you have a valid payment method for your new country. Also, ensure any memberships, pre-orders, or rentals are completed before making the change.
  2. On iPhone/iPad. Open Settings > Apple ID (Your name) > Media & Purchases > View Account > Country/Region > Change Country or Region. Select your new country, review the Terms & Conditions, and update your payment information.
  3. On Mac/PC. Open Apple Music or Apple TV, go to Account > Account Settings (Mac) or View My Account (PC), then click Change Country or Region. Follow the instructions to update your payment details.
  4. Online. Visit account.apple.com, go to Personal Information > Country/Region, and follow the prompts to complete the update.

Remember to cancel any subscriptions, spend your balance, and wait for pending transactions to clear before attempting to change your country or region. For more details, check out the guide from ZoogVPN or Apple support to change your Apple Store regions with ease.

How to check for DNS leaks when connected to VPN?

A DNS leak occurs when your device uses your ISP’s DNS servers instead of the secure DNS servers provided by your VPN, potentially exposing your online activity. To ensure your connection is private and secure, it’s important to check for DNS leaks while connected to ZoogVPN.

Here’s how you can test for DNS leaks:

  1. Run the DNS leak test. Visit ZoogVPN’s DNS Leak Test page. The test will display the DNS servers your device is using.
  2. Analyze the results. If you see DNS servers listed that belong to your ISP or any entity other than ZoogVPN, it indicates a DNS leak. Ideally, only the VPN server’s DNS information should appear.

If you detect a DNS leak, it’s crucial to address the issue to maintain your privacy. Follow the step-by-step guide provided on our DNS leak fix page to resolve the problem effectively.

We at ZoogVPN provide secure DNS servers and leak prevention measures, so you can enjoy a truly private browsing experience. Remember to perform regular tests to ensure your connection remains secure.

When you run ZoogVPN, Windows prompts for permission because it requires administrator access. This security feature protects your system. If you find it inconvenient, you can disable these prompts:

  4. Restart your PC to apply the changes.

Please keep in mind: Disabling UAC notifications lowers your system’s security level. Only do this if you trust all applications on your PC and are comfortable managing your system’s safety manually.

Not able to connect on Linux with OpenVPN client, what should I do?

There is a known issue affecting the OpenVPN client on certain Linux distributions, which has been reported to OpenVPN and is currently pending a fix. In the meantime, you can address this by manually updating your DNS configuration. To do so, follow these steps:

  1. Open a terminal on your Linux system.
  2. Edit the resolv.conf file by running the following command:
    sudo nano /etc/resolv.conf
  3. Remove all existing content in the file and replace it with the following line:
    nameserver 208.67.222.222
  4. Save the changes and exit the editor.
